7.3 Direct update of breeding values

  Preceding page

As a basic rule for direct updating 3 types of information on an animal breeding value exist; the parents, own performance and the offspring. The pieces of information derived from half and full sibs as well as from grandparents are all included in the parent's breeding value. This is both a strength and a weakness in the direct updating method as the parents can only be included once. Therefore, new information on half and full sibs cannot be utilized. Usually this is unimportant, so it is an advantage in several cases not to bother about this information.

The information, gathered from the 3 different sources for the use of estimating the breeding value, has the following forms. The heritability is h2 and common environmental variance c2. The estimated breeding value on an absolute scale has the symbol A hat. An example of this condition: an index has been made of full sib offspring and is included in the combination index. A new index of additional full sibs cannot be included without violating the rule, as they are related to the sibs in the first index, through both the father and the mother. Half sib offspring can, on the other hand, be added when new information arrives, since they are only related through the animal in question.
